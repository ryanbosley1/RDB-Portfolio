The competition's goal was to implement a robot that can successfully mine lunar soil simulant, maneuver rugged terrain with the simulant, and deposit the simulant into the form of a berm.

Harrisburg University also participated in STEM Outreach events, collaborating with local high schools from the Harrisburg area to promote STEM and NASA's mission. The team placed first in the presentation and demonstration category for first year teams, as well as an honorable mention for the STEM Engagement Report.
